My First Site

In game leader and manager of CS team

I played in a semi professional Counter Strike team for a little under 2 years. As I was the only one in the team who had serious sports background, a lot of the admistrative responsibilities fell on me. On top of my in-game responsibilities, I handled our recruitment, sponsorhips and keeping the team together as kind of "coach". The latest of the three was especially challenging, as I had to hold together a team of 5 young and competitive guys with very different personalities in a highly stressfull environment. I learned quite a bit about people management during that time and how to get different personalities to complement each other instead of tearing each toher apart. Although not all of it is applicable in work life, I work a lot better in a team than I did before. For the recruitment part, we changed 2 players during the team's exitence, on top of the 2 players we had to find when establishing a team, and for these I went through over 100 applications and interviewed around 30 potential players in total, of which I suggested 2 or 3 for the rest of the team to consider as our next player for each position.

Key Takeaways

  • Learned people management skills
  • Worked under high pressure
